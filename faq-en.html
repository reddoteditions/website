FAQ

participation

Is the invitation to publish a photograph of my own authorship?

Yes. Each participant will publish a single photograph of their own authorship, selected together during curation.

Can I submit more than one photograph?

Yes, for evaluation during curation. In the publication, however, each participant is represented by a single image.

curation

How will the photograph be selected?

Through dialogue between the participant and the curators, considering the author’s trajectory and the editorial construction of the publication.

Who is part of the curation?

Curation is led by Ale Ruaro, in direct dialogue with each participant throughout the selection process.

Can photographs be declined by the curators?

Curation works in dialogue with each participant. If the image initially proposed is not considered suitable, other options may be presented during the process. The goal is to build the book together, not to exclude invited participants.

publication

How will my work be presented in the book?

Each participant is represented by a single photograph, accompanied by authorship identification.

Will my photograph appear next to another participant’s photograph?

No. Each photograph occupies an exclusive page. The facing page is reserved for editorial elements, such as authorship identification or other content defined during graphic design development.

How should I send the file?

For curation, in low resolution (72 dpi), for viewing only. After selection, a high-resolution file will be requested (TIFF or JPEG, in RGB), suitable for print. Full specifications are sent after the graphic design is defined; conversion to CMYK is done at that stage.

Are the book format, paper, and graphic specifications already defined?

No. Format, page count, paper, finishing, and other specifications are defined after curation is complete, based on the selected set of photographs.

Will there be graphic design approval or printed proofs for participants?

No. The graphic design is developed after curation and led centrally by art direction. Given the number of participants, there is no individual approval or printed proofs — which ensures editorial consistency and makes the production schedule viable.

Where will the book be printed?

Printing will be done by Ipsis.

What will the print run be?

Between 500 and 1,000 copies, depending on demand for additional copies.

Will the book be distributed?

Yes. In addition to sales to the public, copies will go to libraries, museums, cultural institutions, curators, researchers, and photography professionals in Brazil and abroad.

When will I receive my copy?

After the launch and the start of distribution. The shipping schedule will be communicated to participants throughout the project.

Can this edition be reprinted?

No. It is a single edition. red dot editions may develop new publications in the future, with other themes and participants.

investment

What is included in the participation fee?

Curation, editorial production, graphic design, file preparation for print, printing, institutional distribution, national and international shipping, publication launch, and one copy of the book.

Will there be any cost beyond the participation fee?

No. The only optional cost is pre-ordering additional copies.

Can I purchase additional copies?

Yes. Participants may purchase additional copies in advance for R$ 70.00 each, through pre-order.

Will I receive royalties on book sales?

No. The publication follows a cooperative model, without royalty distribution. Sales revenue supports the circulation of the publication and the editorial project.

copyright

Do I retain copyright of the photograph?

Yes, in full. red dot editions receives only the authorization, set out in contract, to reproduce the photograph in the publication and in related promotional materials.

Can I use the published photograph in other projects or publications?

Yes. Participation does not restrict the use of the image in exhibitions, portfolios, publications, or future projects.

Am I responsible for image-use authorization of people portrayed?

Yes. Each participant must ensure they have the necessary authorizations for publication of the photograph, when applicable.
