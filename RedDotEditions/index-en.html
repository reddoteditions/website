edition #001

first edition of red dot editions

A collective publication of contemporary photography, bringing together 100 authors around a single theme: portrait.

about the edition

The curatorial approach starts from an expanded idea of the genre: contemporary photography recognizes that presence does not depend on a face. Silhouettes, gestures, objects, and empty spaces also build a portrait, as in the work of Sophie Calle and Francesca Woodman, or in spaces photographed after an absence. This understanding, present in the portrait tradition from Dorothea Lange and Irving Penn, guides the selection of photographs for this publication.

The publication brings together 100 invited participants, representing different trajectories and approaches to contemporary photography. Each is represented by a single photograph, selected together with the curators based on the author’s trajectory and the editorial construction of the book.

participation

The publication will be produced under a cooperative model: each participant’s contribution funds the editorial production of the edition.

The contribution includes:

  • • Curation;
  • • Editorial production;
  • • Graphic design;
  • • File preparation;
  • • Printing;
  • • Institutional distribution;
  • • National and international shipping;
  • • Publication launch.

circulation

The launch is planned to take place in São Paulo.

The publication will also circulate at major photography festivals in Brazil and through institutional distribution in Brazil and abroad, with copies destined for libraries, museums, cultural institutions, curators, researchers, and photography professionals.

Institutions included in the circulation plan:

  • • Instituto Moreira Salles (IMS)
  • • Pinacoteca de São Paulo
  • • Museu de Arte de São Paulo (MASP)
  • • Museu de Arte Moderna de São Paulo (MAM)
  • • Biblioteca Mário de Andrade
  • • Biblioteca Nacional (Rio de Janeiro)
  • • MEP (Paris)
  • • Bibliothèque nationale de France
  • • Fundação Oriente
  • • Halftone (China)

* The list above represents the editorial goal for institutional circulation and may change depending on availability and logistics.

Approximately 60 copies will be delivered directly to curators, researchers, and photography professionals in Brazil, Argentina, France, and China.
